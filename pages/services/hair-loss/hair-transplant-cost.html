Why Hair Transplantation?

Many of us take our plentiful hair for granted until it’s gone. For most people, a hair transplant can bring back the youthful look they once had. Countless products have promised them to increase volume and help them grow more hair but only on a temporary basis. However, before you choose a specific hair restoration procedure, it is important you study the costs. There is not a simple answer to this question. Everything comes with a price. Treatment and recovery are the two main factors that will affect the total hair transplant cost together with other additional fees we are about to cover in this article.

How Much Does Hair Transplant Cost?

Your hair transplant cost is calculated based on the harvesting technique used, whether FUE or FUT, amount of grafts and individual hair replacement needs of each patient. Online or over-the-phone estimates may not reflect the precise hair transplant cost. That’s why we encourage our patients to schedule a no-obligation consultation with one of our hair specialists. We will create a hair loss prevention plan in line with your aesthetic goals and individual needs. Some factors to consider include:

  • The degree of hair loss you are experiencing
  • Availability of donor hair
  • The particulars of your hair such as texture, color, density, etc.
  • The expected results

The patient’s individual needs will determine the hair restoration cost. There is a specific amount of grafts required for hair restoration. Some patients may require multiple procedures to accomplish optimal results while others may only need to cover a few balding patches in one hair restoration session. The more hair follicles are transplanted during one individual session, the lower the total hair transplant cost per graft will be. In other words, 1,200 grafts will cost the same as 600 grafts as long as it is performed in one session. Breaking up multiple sessions can add extra costs linked to medical equipment, prescription medications, and other administrative costs. However, various sessions will often help with the extraction of more grafts. Again, we encourage you to schedule a free consultation with one of our hair specialists to decide the procedure you need to reach your hair restoration goals and calculate the hair restoration prices.

Is A Hair Transplant Worth The Cost?

Losing your hair, particularly at a young age, can be a difficult thing to go through. For many people, paying for hair restoration surgery may not be a sacrifice while for others; it may be too great of a cost so they will opt for temporary solutions such as hairpieces and medical treatments. While temporary options may be financially reachable for the patient, it’s also vital to consider the hair transplantation cost and long-term effects of these non-surgical options.

How To Calculate The Cost Of Your Hair Transplant

The hair transplant cost is determined based on the hair transplant cost per graft. Each graft has 1-4 hair follicles, so the amount of grafts each patient needs is based on your expectations and goals, which is usually 600 for patients experiencing less hair loss and 3,000 for those that are suffering from massive hair loss. Often, the more hair grafts transplanted per session, the lower the hair transplant prices will be.

The Costs Of FUT Vs. FUE

Thanks to the availability of these two techniques, hair doctors can now extract and transplant several follicular units at once. It’s all based on the number of hair follicles obtained from the donor area. While FUE is a more advanced and less invasive technique, it’s more expensive since the physician must remove each hair follicle individually. FUE can be time-consuming. FUT, on the other hand, allows the removal of the greatest number of grafts in a single session. The cost can vary by patient’s conditions, needs, and objectives. However, as FUE becomes more popular, the prices will be lower so that the cost difference between the two procedures is much less.
